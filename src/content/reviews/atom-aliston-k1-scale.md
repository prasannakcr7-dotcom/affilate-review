## Our Verdict
The ATOM ALISTON K1 Series Digital Kitchen Scale is Aliston's budget-friendly food scale. It is built for weighing kitchen ingredients and competes directly with the Btag SF-400 and the CRAE SF-400. Overall, it is a practical choice for home cooks seeking a high-capacity scale at a low price point, though its plastic housing is basic and it does not display fractional grams.

## Measurement Accuracy & Capacity
The scale is equipped with high-precision sensors and supports a weight capacity from 1 gram up to 10 kg. It measures in both grams and ounces, accommodating a variety of culinary ingredients. According to customer feedback, the measurements are generally consistent for basic household cooking and baking.

However, multiple buyers report that the scale does not display fractions of a gram, measuring only in whole-gram increments. For tasks requiring extreme precision, such as measuring spices or small yeast portions, this limitation makes it less suitable. The accuracy can also vary slightly under regular daily use, but it remains adequate for general meal prep.

## Design, Build & Backlit Display
The device features a compact design with a white plastic body. The build quality receives mixed reviews from buyers. Multiple reviews note that the plastic housing feels basic and thin, which may affect long-term durability. Some buyers also report that the color of the unit they received differed from the listing details.

For reading measurements, the scale has an LCD screen with an automatic backlight. According to buyer reviews, this display remains easy to read in low-light kitchen conditions. The control buttons are positioned below the screen and respond to light pressure, though some users report that the button click mechanism feels cheap.

## Tare Function & Power Source
The scale includes a physical tare button to simplify multi-ingredient measurements. Tapping the button resets the display to zero, subtracting the weight of any container or bowl placed on the weighing platform. This allows users to add and weigh multiple ingredients in a single bowl consecutively.

The unit runs on two AA batteries, which are included in the box. The manufacturer does not state the average battery life, but buyer reviews indicate that the power consumption is low and the batteries last for several months of regular kitchen usage. The battery compartment is located on the underside of the scale and does not require tools to open.

## Should You Buy It?
The ATOM ALISTON K1 Series Scale is a good choice for budget buyers who want a simple, high-capacity digital scale for portion tracking or baking. It handles tare calculations and basic weight measurements well, and the ₹249 price point is lower than most options in the category. The main limitations are the basic plastic build and the lack of decimal/fractional gram readings. If those are not concerns for your daily use, it is worth considering. If you need a sturdier build or stainless steel platform, look at the Tata 1mg Weightwise Scale instead.
