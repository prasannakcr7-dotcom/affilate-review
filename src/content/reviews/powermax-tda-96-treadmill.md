## Our Verdict
The PowerMax Fitness TDA-96 is PowerMax Fitness's budget-friendly treadmill. It is built for home workouts and competes directly with the Fitkit Pulse Run Pro and the Lifelong FitPro Treadmill. Overall, it is a functional choice for walking and jogging at home, though it is limited by a narrow belt and a short continuous motor running time.

## Motor Performance & Speed
The treadmill is equipped with a 4.0 HP Peak DC motor. The manufacturer states that the motor operates under 50 decibels, which keeps noise to a minimum. However, the DC motor has a strict duty cycle. The listing specifies that the motor can run continuously for up to 30 minutes, after which it requires a 20-minute break to cool down.

According to customer feedback, the motor transitions between speeds smoothly without lagging. The speed range extends up to 12 km/h, which is sufficient for light jogging but does not support sprinting. Because of the duty cycle limit, the treadmill is unsuitable for households where multiple people plan to exercise consecutively without a break.

## Auto-Incline & Programs
A key feature of the treadmill is its 15% automatic incline system. Adjustments to the incline level can be made using buttons on the console while walking or jogging. This allows users to increase training intensity without needing to step off the machine to make manual adjustments.

The console is preloaded with 12 workout programs that automatically adjust speed and incline to simulate different terrains. Buyers report that these preset routines are simple to activate and help structure daily exercise sessions. The incline adjustment mechanism operates quietly and does not disrupt the belt's movement.

## Design, Folding & Running Deck
The treadmill features a hydraulic soft-drop folding mechanism, which allows the deck to lower to the floor hands-free. When folded, the machine occupies less than one square meter of floor space, making it suitable for smaller rooms. Transport wheels on the frame help with relocation. The frame supports a maximum user weight of 110 kg and weighs 33 kg.

The running belt measures 105 cm in length and 38 cm in width, and uses a five-layer non-slip texture. Multiple buyer reviews note that the running surface is narrow. Some users describe the belt area as cramped, particularly for running. Additionally, users taller than 155 cm or those with long strides report feeling restricted by the short belt length, indicating that the deck size is better suited for walking rather than running.

## Console & Smart Features
The control panel features an LED display that shows workout metrics including speed, distance, time, and calories burned. Heart rate sensors are integrated into the lower handrails. However, buyer reviews note that the sensors are positioned awkwardly low on the frame, requiring the user to bend down to hold them while walking.

The treadmill includes Bluetooth connectivity, allowing users to play music through the built-in speakers and connect to fitness tracking apps. A magnetic safety clip is attached to the console and clips to the user's clothing. If the runner stumbles and detaches the clip, the treadmill stops the belt instantly. While buyers appreciate the safety clip and app connectivity, some reviews mention that the built-in speaker quality is basic.

## Should You Buy It?
The PowerMax Fitness TDA-96 is a good choice for buyers who want an automatic incline treadmill for walking or jogging in a limited space. It handles basic fitness tracking and folding well, and the automatic incline feature is better than most manual incline options at this price. The main limitations are the narrow 38 cm belt width and the 30-minute continuous motor limit. If those are not concerns for your use, it is worth considering. If you need a wider running surface or a longer continuous run time, look at the Fitkit Pulse Run Pro instead.
