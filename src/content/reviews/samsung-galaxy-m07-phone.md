## Our Verdict
The Samsung Galaxy M07 is Samsung's budget-friendly entry-level smartphone. It is built for everyday usage and competes directly with the Realme Narzo 80 Lite and the Lava Bold N2. Overall, it is a practical option for buyers seeking long-term software support and durability on a budget, though it lacks an included charger and a high-resolution display.

## Performance & Software Updates
The smartphone is powered by a MediaTek Helio G99 processor with 4GB of RAM and 64GB of internal storage. The manufacturer claims this setup achieves an AnTuTu benchmark score of 624K. According to customer feedback, the device runs smoothly and handles basic daily tasks such as web browsing, messaging, and social media without lagging. However, some buyers note that the phone can feel slightly slow when loading larger files or running demanding applications.

Samsung rates this device for six generations of Android OS upgrades and six years of security updates, which is longer than most competing phones in this price category. The phone runs Android 15 with Samsung's One UI 7.0 platform. To protect data, the device integrates Knox Vault hardware-level security, built-in theft protection, and an auto blocker feature.

## Display & Slim Design
The device features a 6.7-inch display with HD+ resolution (720x1600 pixels) and a 90Hz refresh rate. While the 90Hz refresh rate makes scrolling animations appear smoother, the HD+ resolution is relatively low for a screen of this size. As a result, text and graphics do not look as sharp as they would on a Full HD screen.

The chassis measures 7.6 mm in thickness and features a glass fiber reinforced polymer back panel. This design keeps the phone slim and relatively lightweight. For durability, the phone is rated IP54 for dust and water protection, making it resistant to splashes and light rain.

## Camera Output
The rear camera array consists of a 50-megapixel primary sensor (f/1.8) and a 2-megapixel depth helper sensor. The front-facing camera features an 8-megapixel sensor (f/2.0). The maximum video resolution supported for recording is Full HD (1920x1080).

According to customer feedback, the rear camera captures decent pictures in daylight with acceptable color and detail for an entry-level phone. However, low-light performance is limited, showing visible noise. The front camera is sufficient for basic video calls and selfies, though details are less refined.

## Battery Life & Charging
The smartphone is equipped with a 5000 mAh battery. The manufacturer rates the device for 25W fast charging via a USB Type-C port. Buyer reviews indicate that the battery capacity is a major strength, regularly providing more than a full day of backup under typical usage patterns.

However, the phone is sold without a wall charger, and the box only contains a USB-C to USB-C cable. Buyers who do not already own a compatible 25W USB-C power adapter will need to buy one separately. Additionally, multiple buyers report that the included data cable is unreliable, sometimes failing to charge the phone properly or requiring replacement.

## Should You Buy It?
The Samsung Galaxy M07 is a good choice for budget buyers who want a slim phone with long software support and a large battery. It handles basic messaging, calling, and media consumption well, and the six-year update policy is better than most options under ₹11,000. The main limitations are the low-resolution screen and the lack of an included wall charger. If those are not concerns for your daily use, it is worth considering. If you need a sharper screen or want a charger in the box, look at the Realme Narzo 80 Lite instead.
