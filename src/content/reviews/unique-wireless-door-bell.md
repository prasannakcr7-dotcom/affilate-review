## Our Verdict
The UN1QUE Wireless Doorbell (U400B) is UN1QUE's budget-friendly wireless calling bell. It's built for homes and offices and competes directly with wireless bells from GM and Costar. Overall, it's a convenient choice for users wanting an easy setup without wiring, though configuring its chimes is tedious and long-term reliability is mixed.

## Wireless Range & Signal Penetration
The manufacturer claims that this wireless doorbell has an operating range of up to 300 meters (1000 feet) in open areas. In real-world indoor settings, buyer feedback indicates that the signal propagates well through rooms and walls. Multiple users report a reliable connection over a distance of approximately 50 meters, making it suitable for larger houses and multi-story offices. 

However, structural barriers such as thick concrete walls or metal frames can reduce the maximum range. For standard apartment or small office layouts, the wireless transmitter maintains a stable connection with the receiver without signal drops.

## Chime Selection & Volume Control
The doorbell kit offers 36 selectable chimes, ranging from traditional ding-dong sounds to longer melodies. Volume is adjustable across four levels, spanning from 20dB up to 100dB at maximum output. According to customer reviews, the highest volume setting is loud enough to be heard clearly from other rooms or in noisy environments. 

A main limitation is the tune selection interface. The receiver features only one button to configure the sound, forcing users to cycle forward through all 36 chimes to find a preferred melody. If you accidentally pass the desired chime, you must press the button to cycle through the entire list again.

## Design, Info Board & Waterproofing
The unit features a modern, compact design and includes a flashing LED light on the receiver that blinks when the doorbell rings. This visual indicator is useful for hearing-impaired users or when the volume is set to low. The transmitter also features a write-on info board area where users can slide in a paper note to leave instructions for visitors or delivery agents.

The push-button transmitter is rated IP44 waterproof, which provides protection against water splashes. While this is sufficient for covered entrances and light rain, it is not fully weatherproof and can fail if exposed to heavy downpours without an overhead cover. Additionally, some buyers note that the remote push button is very small and feels lightweight.

## Power Source & Installation
The system uses a plug-and-play design. The smart receiver plugs directly into any standard AC wall socket, while the outdoor push button runs on battery power. The transmitter and receiver are paired out of the box, allowing immediate use. 

For mounting the transmitter, the package includes both double-sided adhesive tape and mounting screws. Buyers report that installing the push button using the adhesive tape takes less than a minute and adheres firmly to flat wooden or metal frames. However, screws are recommended for rough masonry walls to prevent the transmitter from falling.

## Should You Buy It?
The UN1QUE Wireless Doorbell is a good choice for budget buyers who need a simple, wireless calling bell for a flat or office. It handles signal range well, and the 100dB maximum volume is louder than most other models at this price. The main limitations are the tedious single-button melody configuration and the mixed long-term reliability reports. If those are not concerns for your home, it is a good value. If you need a more durable outdoor unit with more melodies, look at the GM Strio Wireless Doorbell instead.
